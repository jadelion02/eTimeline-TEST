引言

幾年前，在看了幾集關於14 世紀的著名中國探險家和航海家鄭和(明朝)的電視節目後，我問自己幾個問題：耶穌的時代是哪個中國皇帝在座？亞伯拉罕的時代是中國什麼王朝？經過幾分鐘的思考，我發現自己無法回答這兩個簡單的問題。

今天，聯網技術和服務在整個世界範圍內形成了一個全球通信網絡，信息可以快速地跨越文化和地理的鴻溝。在我們沒有這技術的情況下，在探索時代(13 至 16 世紀)期間，由於地理和文化鴻溝，文明相對孤立。然而，我們有一個共同而持久的因素或衡量標準支配著這個世界，那就是"時間"，所有過去和現在的文明都可以在這個”時間”中相遇。

我開始在網上閱讀歷史和研究在網上的資料，以能找到我最初問題的答案。像大多數人一樣，我的研究激起了我對其他聖經和歷史人物和事件的興趣，例如大洪水。最初，為了我自己清晰起見，我使用電子表格在時間軸上佈置我的研究。但不久之後，當我擴大我的研究範圍並收集更多資料時，我發現電子表格方法概述各種古代文明/歷史的時間線之間的關係和交叉點的不足。經過進一步研究，我發現我可以使用超文本標記語言進行編程，以創建一個更合適的工具，以有組織的方式顯示我的大量資料，並為讀者提供更多在網上的資料。正如其他人所說......其餘的都是歷史。

在構造我的"電子歷史時間線"時，我發現這是一個有趣的旅程，有時也充滿挑戰。除了技術挑戰之外，由於我不是專業的程序員，也不是受過培訓的程序員，因此其他挑戰必須：

1) 從歷史記錄、各種不同的文明和歷史學家中提取有關歷史人物和事件的信息，而製定的獨特日曆。在許多情況下，在一個文明的時期內可能有多個統治者同時統治，例如埃及、中國等。此外，由於不同歷史學家的不同解釋，多個相互矛盾記錄的存在，導致有一些不確定性的爭議或矛盾。

2) 整理各種不同的歷史記錄，並以更按時間順序、連貫性和簡潔易讀的形式呈現它們，以便可以沿著共同的時間線查看著名人物和歷史事件。

我的"電子歷史時間線"由三個獨立的時間線組成，即：創造時間線、歷史時間線和耶穌基督的啟示。請注意，前面提到的最後一條時間線只是沿時間軸佈置《啟示錄》中記錄的預言事件的嘗試。

隨著我的項目從一個簡單的電子表格演變為一組更複雜、內容更豐富的時間表，以下是對形成電子表格的一些想法和考慮因素的進一步解釋，這些想法和考慮因素有助於指導時間表的構建。如我發現更多有趣的歷史或重要人物和事件將被包含在未來，這時間線將會繼續發展。

1. 宇宙的年齡

聖經在創世記中說，天地是上帝在6天內創造或說成的，但科學研究估計宇宙的年齡約為140億年。關於我們的宇宙有多年紀，有幾種不同的理論、觀點和解釋，其中包括：文字學和文學解釋、日齡理論、間隙理論、啟示日理論和兩視同實理論等。 .

在古代，宇宙被認為是"靜止的"和"永恆的"，是沒有開始的。然而，對於我們關於宇宙年齡的討論，我們必須考慮到宇宙應有一個開端。現代科學研究發現了大爆炸的迴聲，並得出結論，宇宙確實有一個開端。除了對年齡的不同解釋之外，意見的顯著分歧在於宇宙是非常年輕（大約 6,000 到 10,000 年）或是非常古老（大約 140 億年）。

正如我在"電子歷史時間線"中所描述的那樣，創造時間線基於 Gerald L. Schroeder 博士提出的兩視同實理論。他的第一個觀點是宇宙的開始是於宇宙時間的開始，這是基於 6 天的創造，到亞當從塵土中被造出來，有一個靈魂，並具有上帝的形象和樣式。這個觀點是從開始宇宙只是一個小斑點的角度來看的，並展望未來宇宙繼續伸展和膨脹。根據科學觀察，空間伸展時，時間也伸展。在亞當被創造時，宇宙時間變為地球時間。施羅德博士的第二個時間觀，從開始，當我們回溯到宇宙最開始時，大約是 140 億年。兩種觀點都被認為是正確的，這取決於一個人是從頭向前看到現在，還是從現在向後看。有關更多詳細信息，請參閱 Schroeder 博士關於宇宙年齡 (Age of the Universe) 的文章。

2. 歷史上的日曆

根據希伯來時間推算，年份從公元前 3761 年(基督之前)或公元前(或共同時代之前)開始。這種做法是在 12 世紀實施的，當時猶太哲學家邁蒙尼德 (Maimonides) 將時間框架確定為聖經中的創世日期。當前的希伯來歷是基於公元二世紀(共同紀元或現稱為公元)拉比何塞(或約西)本哈拉夫塔 (Rabbi Jose ben Halafta) (希伯來語：רבי יוסי בן חלפתא) 的著作。何塞被認為是 Seder Olam Rabba 的作者，這是一部從創造到哈德良 (Hadrian) 時代的編年史。這個希伯來歷是以色列的官方曆法，也是用於猶太宗教中的儀式。

歷史上和現在，有許多不同的日曆和方法來測量時間的流逝，例如阿茲特克和瑪雅日曆，以及基於聖經七十士譯本的更現代的拜占庭日曆，以及亞瑟年表 (The Ussher Chronology)。世界年鑑 (James Ussher，Amagh 大主教)等。歷史上的統治者，例如皇帝、法老、皇室等傾向於根據他們的個人以統治者身份建造自己的日曆。目前，格里高利歷和儒略歷在各大洲使用最為廣泛。

歷史時間線，如我在"電子歷史時間線"中所描述的，基於當前的希伯來日曆，並使用幾個錨年來沿著時間線佈置重要的歷史和當前人物、人物和事件。第一個錨定年份是亞當被創造時的希伯來日歷第 1 年 (HC - 即公元前 3761 年)。第二個錨定年份是希伯來日歷2448 年 (即公元前 1313 年)，即以色列人在"埃及地和迦南地"逗留 430 年後離開埃及(即出埃及)的那一年 (七十士譯本出埃及記 12:40)。在這兩個錨定年份之外發生的可能是由於從一個日曆轉換到另一個日曆，或由於不清楚、不完整和不准確的記錄。

與希伯來歷相似，中國歷是根據月亮和太陽的運動計算的陰陽曆。公曆是通過太陽的運動來計算的；地球繞太陽轉一圈就是一年。一般來說，陰陽曆比公曆晚20到50天。中國曆法的歷史可以追溯到夏朝 (公元前 21 至 16 世紀) 和商朝 (公元前 16 至 11 世紀)。它已經存在了 4,000 多年，其基礎是通過觀察和探索將天文學和地理獨特地結合起來。

縱觀中國歷史，保存朝廷編年史是一種慣例。司馬遷的《史記》，被認為是中國歷史上最重要的記載。還有其他著名的中國歷史學家，如孔子、左丘明和司馬談等。中國朝代、皇帝、事件和人物以及其他文明的一些資料和鏈接是從公開文獻和免費內容中收集的百科全書。

中國曆法是基於所謂的天干地支。它是一個六十期的周期，每一期對應一年，因此一個週期總共是六十年。它在歷史上用於記錄中國和大多數東亞文化的時間。將周期年轉換為等效的公曆年需要進行若干解釋性數學計算。由於沒有普遍認可的轉換指南可用，計算並不總是提供準確的轉換。此外，歷史學家可能對正確的時間框架有自己的解釋。雖然日曆的同步提供了統一的歷史視圖，但由於在材料中來源的分歧，總會有一些事件、人物和與其他時間框架不同步，達到數十年甚至數百年的時間。

在其他文獻中發現年齡的差異，例如 馬所拉文本 (Masoretic Text), 撒瑪利亞摩西五經 (Samaritan Pentateuch), 七十士譯本 (Septuagint (LXX)) 和 約瑟夫斯的猶太古史(Anitiquities of the Jews by Flavius Josephus) 關於他們頭生出生時的年齡（或根據彌賽亞祖先的說法）差異很大，從幾百年到一千年不等。 以下表列出各個年齡的詳細資料:

Introduction

A few years ago, after watching few television episodes on Zeng He (鄭和 - the famous Chinese explorer and mariner who lived in the 14th century of the Ming dynasty (明朝)), I asked myself a couple of questions: which Chinese emperor was on the throne during the time of Jesus, and what was the Chinese dynasty during the time of Abraham? After few minutes of pondering, I found myself unable to answer these two simple questions.

Today the availability of the world-wide internet technologies and services cast a global communication net around the entire world, where information can be disseminated and exchanged quickly across cultural and geographical divides. In the absence of our current technologies and before the age of explorations (13th – 16th centuries), civilizations were relatively isolated due to geographic and cultural divides. However, I recognize that there is one common and persistent factor or measurement that governs this world, and that is ‘time’ in which all past and present civilizations existed/exists in parallel and can intersect by it.

I started by reading history books and researching online resources to find answer/information to my original questions. Like most people, my researches piqued my interest on other biblical and historical characters and events, such as the great flood. Initially, for my own clarity, I used a spreadsheet to layout my research on a timeline. But shortly after, as I expanded my original scope and gathered more information, I found the spreadsheet approach was insufficient in providing an overview of the relationships and intersections of the timelines from various ancient civilizations/histories. After further research, I discovered that I can program in hypertext markup language to create a more appropriate tool to display my copious amount of information in an organized manner, and also to provide the ability for viewers to access more open-sourced information on the internet. As they say … the rest is history.

In constructing the ‘eTimeline’, I find it to be an interesting, and at times, challenging journey. Aside from the technical challenges, as I am not a programmer by profession nor by training, the other challenges are having to:

1) Extract information on relevant historical persons/characters and events by from historical records, from various disparate civilizations and historians, that are often based on unique calendars established by individual ruler specific for their reign. In many cases, there could be multiple rulers reigning concurrently during a period in one civilization, e.g. Egyptian, Chinese, etc. Additionally, there are multiple contradictory records based on different interpretations by various historians, resulting in some uncertainties and/or disputes/contradictions.

2) Synchronize and organize various and disparate historical records and presenting them in a more chronological, cohesive and concise legible manner so that notable persons/characters and historical events can be viewed along a common timeline.

The ‘eTimeline’ consists of three separate timelines, namely: eTimeline of Creation, eTimeline of History, and eTimeline of Revelation. Please note that the last aforementioned timeline is only an attempt to layout prophesized events, as recorded in the book of Revelation, along a time axis

As my project evolved from a simple spreadsheet to a set of more complexed and content-rich timelines, below are further explanations on some of the thoughts and considerations that form the guide posts which helped to steer the construction of the timelines. The timelines will continue to evolve as I find more interesting historical and current persons/characters and events to be included in the future.

1. Age of the Universe

The Bible states, in Genesis, that the heaven and earth were created, or spoken into being by God, in 6 days, however scientific studies have estimated the age of the universe to be about 14 billion years. There are several different theories, opinions and interpretations of how old our universe is, among them are: The Literal interpretation and the Literary interpretation, the Day-Age theory, the Gap theory, the Revelatory Day theory and the Two-View One-Reality theory, etc.

In ancient times, the universe was considered to be “static" and “eternal" which has no beginning. However, for our discussion on the age of the universe, we must consider that the universe has a beginning. Modern scientific studies have discovered the echo of the Big Bang and concluded that the universe indeed had a beginning. Aside from the different interpretations of the age, the notable divergence of opinion is whether the universe is either very young (about 6,000 to 10,000 years) or extremely old (about 14 billion years).

The Creation Timeline, as described in the ‘eTimeline’, is based on the Two-View One-Reality theory proposed by Dr. Gerald L. Schroeder. His first view is that cosmic time started with the beginning of the universe which is based on the 6-day of creation, to the creation of Adam out of dust, with a soul, and in the image and likeness of God. This view is taken from the viewpoint at the very beginning when the universe was just a minuscule speck, and looking forward in time into the future where the universe continues stretching and expanding thereafter. According to scientific observations, when space stretches out, time also stretches out as well. At the creation of Adam, the frame of reference or perspective changes from cosmic time to earth time. Dr. Schroeder’s second view of time, since the beginning, is about 14 billion years when we look backwards in time to the very beginning of the universe. Both views are considered correct, depending on if one is looking forward from the beginning to the present, or is looking backwards from the present to the beginning. Refer to the article on the Age of the Universe by Dr. Schroeder for more details.

2. Calendars in History

According to Hebrew time reckoning, the year count starts in the year 3761 BC (Before Christ) or BCE (Before Common Era). The practice was implemented in the 12th-century when Jewish philosopher Maimonides established the timeframe as the biblical date of Creation. The current Hebrew calendar is based on the writing of Rabbi Jose (or Yossi) ben Halafta (Hebrew: רבי יוסי בן חלפתא) of the second century CE (Common Era or currently known as AD (Anno Domini)). Jose is considered to be the author of the Seder Olam Rabba, a chronicle from the creation to the time of Hadrian. This Hebrew calendar is the official calendar of Israel, and is being used for Jewish religious observances.

Historically and currently, there are many different calendars and methods to measure the passage of time, such as the Aztec and the Mayan calendars, and the more modern Byzantine calendar which is based on the Septuagint text of the Bible, and the Ussher Chronology (The Annals of the World by James Ussher, the Archbishop of Amagh and Primate of all of Ireland), etc. Historically rulers, e.g. emperors, pharaohs, royalties, etc. tend to create their own calendars based on their individual reign. Currently, the Gregorian and the Julian calendars are most widely used across the continents.

The History Timeline, as described in the ‘eTimeline’, is based on the current Hebrew calendar and uses several anchor years for the layout of significant historical and current characters, persons and events along the timeline. The first anchor year is Year 1 HC (Hebrew Calendar - i.e. 3761 BC) when Adam was created. The second anchor year is Year 2448 HC (i.e. 1313 BC), the year in which the Israelis departed from Egypt (i.e. Exodus) after 430 years of sojourn “in the land of Egypt and the land of Canaan" (Septuagint Exodus 12:40). References that occur outside these two anchor years are approximations only due to the conversion from one calendar to another, and/or due to unclear, incomplete, and/or inaccurate records.

Similar to the Hebrew calendar, the Chinese calendar is a lunisolar calendar which is calculated according to the movement of the moon and the sun. The Gregorian calendar is calculated by the movement of the sun; when the earth goes around the sun for one circle, it is one year. Generally speaking, lunisolar calendar is 20 to 50 days later than Gregorian calendar. The Chinese calendar has a very long history going back to the Xia 夏 (21st - 16th century BC) and Shang Dynasty 商朝 (16th - 11th century BC). It has existed for more than 4,000 years and is based on a unique combination of astronomy and geography through observations and explorations.

Throughout Chinese history, the keeping of court chronicles was an established practice. Sima Qian’s (司馬遷) Shiji – Records of the Grand Historian or Historical Records (史記), is considered to be the most significantly important record of Chinese history. There are other notable Chinese historians such as Confucius (孔子), 左丘明 & 司馬談 etc. Some of the information and links to the Chinese dynasties, emperors, events and characters, and other civilizations are gleaned from publicly available literatures and free content encyclopedia.

Chinese calendar is based on what is known as the sexagenary cycle, also known as the Stems and Branches or ganzhi (干支). It is a cycle of sixty terms, each corresponding to one year, thus a total of sixty years for one cycle. It is historically used for recording time in China and most of the East Asian cultures. The conversion of the cyclical year to equivalent Gregorian year requires several interpretive mathematical calculations. As there is no universal recognized conversion guideline available, the calculations do not always offer an accurate conversion/equivalency. In addition, historians may have their own interpretations of the correct timeframe.
